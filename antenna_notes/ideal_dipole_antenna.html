What is an Ideal Dipole Antenna?

A dipole antenna is any antenna that creates a radiation pattern similar to the pattern that would be generated by two charges separated by some distance (in other words: a dipole). Dipole antennas are frequently created using two conductors placed at some angle to each other. For example, the “bunny ears” on older TVs are dipole antennas.

For the purpose of these notes, a dipole antenna will be constructed of two conductors separated from each other by 180°180\degree180°. The antenna will be center-fed.

– PICTURE –

An ideal dipole antenna (or: infinitesimal dipole) is a dipole antenna that has no loss, has a uniform current distribution across its conductors, and has a length much less than the wavelength of radiated wave.
